## Process: Scholarship and Award Member Support

**When this comes up:** Managing the awards inbox, supporting members through applications, participating in judging, or collaborating with internal teams on the NSLS Scholarships & Awards Program.

**Goal:** Deliver a world-class, fair, and CARE-aligned experience for all scholarship applicants.

**Key definitions:**
- **Fully Inducted Member** — Completed Orientation, LTD, 3 Speaker Broadcasts, and 3 SNTs (ceremony not required)
- **Stephen Covey Tribute Awards** — Chapter of the Year, Advisor of the Year, Executive Board Member of the Year; submitted to awards@nsls.org only
- **Scholar's App** — Platform for most scholarship applications and judging
- **Judging Round 1/2/3** — Multi-round review process; Round 1 by MEX Specialists, Round 2 by MEX Leadership, Round 3 by cross-functional volunteers; final decisions by SLT/Foundation
- **W-9** — Required for U.S. citizens receiving NSLS scholarships
- **W-8 BEN** — Required for international students receiving NSLS scholarships

**Step 1 — Prepare:**
- Review all training materials in the MEx Scholarships Drive
- Visit nsls.org/scholarships-and-awards to understand current offerings, eligibility, and dates

**Step 2 — Confirm access and attend judge's training:**
- Log into Scholar's App before review assignments begin
- Attend required judge's training or watch the make-up video

**Step 3 — Inbox and judging expectations:**
- Workforce assigns all emails from awards@nsls.org and scholarship review applications
- SLA for awards inbox: **24 hours** (not the standard 48)
- Use the correct 8x8 status code while judging
- When reviewing multiple scholarship types, complete one full batch before switching
- All assigned award emails and reviews are due **same day they are assigned**
- If unable to complete, contact the Workforce Specialist immediately for reassignment

**Step 4 — Deadline surges:**
- Members may submit up to 11:59 PM EST on the final day — expect a rush
- Extra hours may be offered or required; all MEX Specialists should be ready to support an all-hands effort at close of each cycle

## Process: Scholarship Inquiry Response & Student Encouragement

**When this comes up:** Any scholarship question via phone, chat, text, or Support Pipeline. All MEX Specialists handle these — no need to transfer to the Awards Pipeline.

**Goal:** Respond within 24 hours, encourage members to apply for every scholarship they're eligible for, and inspire them to complete applications by the deadline.

**Key definitions:**
- **NSLS National Scholarships** — Open to all NSLS members regardless of chapter; listed in the current Scholarship & Awards Program Guide
- **Chapter-Specific Scholarships** — Funded and managed by an individual chapter; only open to members of that chapter
- **Stephen Covey Tribute Award** — Honors exceptional character, integrity, and commitment to personal growth aligned with Covey's principles; applications submitted to awards@nsls.org
- **W-9** — Required for U.S. citizens/permanent residents receiving NSLS scholarships
- **W-8 BEN** — Required for non-U.S. residents receiving NSLS scholarships

**Step 1 — Monitor and assign tickets:**
- Continuously monitor tickets throughout the day; self-assign new inquiries when workload is manageable
- Assignments are also distributed by the MEx WorkForce Specialist at 11 AM, 1 PM, 3 PM, and 5 PM
- Do not self-assign tickets belonging to another agent unless directed by MEL

**Before responding — confirm:**
- [ ] Is it a general NSLS scholarship or chapter-specific?
- [ ] What is the member's status (pre-inducted or inducted)?
- [ ] What is the scholarship name or season, if known?
- [ ] Which snippet or email template applies?

**Available email templates:** Scholarship Information, Scholarship Information (LOR), Scholarship Information (Winners), Scholars App Technology Issue (School Not Listed), Scholars App Technology Issue (Edit), Application Past Deadline

**Step 2 — Use the Scholarship PDF Guide:**
- Reference the current **Scholarship & Awards Program Guide** for details on each scholarship; share it with the member or direct them to download it
- For applications: guide the member to the **Benefits Overview** tab → 4th box "Apply for Scholarship" → red "Learn More" button → Scholars App website
- For Scholars App issues: try to resolve first; if unable, share Scholars App contact details:
  - General Support: hello@scholarsapp.com
  - Technical Support: support@scholarsapp.com
  - Phone: (808) 374-4559

**Step 3 — Respond clearly and encourage:**
- Use a warm, encouraging tone — especially for members who missed deadlines
- Encourage members to apply for as many scholarships as they're eligible for
- Use the phrase: *"We're here to support you as you pursue your goals!"*
- Escalate complex inquiries; consider a phone call if needed

**Post-procedure:**
- Follow up within 24 hours if more research is needed
- If SOS is needed, pin a note or set a task in the ticket to stay within SLA

**Common questions:**

*Where are the scholarship criteria and guidelines?*
- In the NSLS Scholarships & Awards Guide. The page is public — scroll past any nomination code prompt to find the guide under "Helping You Reach Your Goals."

*Where is the open scholarship application?*
- On the Scholars App. For an overview, share or download the scholarship PDF.

*Can a member apply for scholarships from a chapter they're not part of?*
- No — chapter-specific scholarships are for members of that chapter only. All NSLS members can apply for national scholarships at nsls.org/scholarships.

*Did I win a scholarship?*
- Winner release dates are in the "Open Scholarship Periods and Deadlines" section of the current Scholarship & Awards Program Guide. Winners are also notified by email and listed under "Featured Scholarship Winners" on the NSLS website.

*Who should write the LOR for a scholarship?*
- A professional, academic, or personal reference who knows the member personally/professionally. **NSLS staff cannot write LORs for NSLS scholarships — it's a conflict of interest.** Inducted members can use their FOL LOR for external (non-NSLS) scholarships.

*Can applications be submitted to awards@nsls.org?*
- **Only for Stephen Covey Tribute Awards** (Chapter of the Year, Advisor of the Year, Executive Board Member of the Year). All other scholarships **must** be submitted through Scholars App — applications emailed for other scholarships will not be reviewed.

*Can the W-9 or W-8 BEN have someone else's name on it?*
- No. The scholarship recipient must enter their own details.

*Do members pay taxes on scholarships?*
- Scholarships may be taxable income. A 1099 form is issued for total scholarships of $600 or more in a calendar year (excludes Academic Excellence Scholarship paid directly to a university). Refer members to IRS guidelines and advise them to consult a tax professional.
