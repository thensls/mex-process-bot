## Process: Refund Exception — MEx Policy

**When this comes up:** A member requests a refund — within or outside the 30-day window — or when handling escalations, save attempts, and exception decisions.

**Core policy:**
- NSLS offers a **30-day refund window** for all new memberships
- Refunds within 30 days are available after at least one documented save attempt
- **No refunds after 30 days** unless a rare exception is approved by MEX Leadership
- Members outside the window may have their account **disabled** but membership fees remain nonrefundable
- Graduation item refunds follow a separate SOP

---

**Step 1 — Acknowledge and reassure:**
- Open with empathy: *"I'm so sorry to hear you're thinking about giving up your membership. Let's take a look at your account to see what options are available — I'm here to help."*
- Commit to exploring all available solutions before making any decision

**Step 2 — Discover the root cause:**
- Ask open-ended questions to understand why the member wants to cancel
- Document the member's response in the HubSpot ticket
- If FOL-related: also log in the **MEx FOL Saves Rate Tracker**

**Step 3 — Tailor the save attempt to the member's specific situation:**

Every member deserves a CARE-based save — and the approach should match *why* they're upset, not a script. Discover the root cause first, then respond to it directly:

- **"I don't have time to complete the steps"** → Discuss online chapter options that fit their schedule; reinforce flexibility of the program
- **"I'm not sure about the value"** → Walk through specific benefits relevant to their goals: scholarships, career tools, discounts, speaker broadcasts, lifetime access
- **"I had a bad experience"** → Acknowledge it, investigate, take ownership of what you can fix
- **"I can't afford it"** → Reinforce this is a one-time fee with lifetime access; highlight the scholarship and career tools that can offset cost
- **All saves:** Celebrate their nomination, ask open-ended questions, document everything in HubSpot

The policy is firm — we hold to the 30-day window — but the save attempt always comes before any policy explanation. Focus the conversation on the member's needs, not the refund.

**Step 4 — Prevent the escalation:**

Your goal is to de-escalate and resolve *before* the member demands a transfer. Guide the conversation with CARE:
- Stay calm and empathetic even if the member is upset
- Don't volunteer policy limits unprompted — address the *problem* first
- If the member asks directly about a refund outside 30 days: acknowledge the policy exists, then redirect to what you *can* do ("What I want to make sure is that we explore every option available to you before we get there")
- Document your save attempt in HubSpot as you go

**Step 5 — If escalation occurs:**

If the member cannot be de-escalated after a full save attempt, hand off to a **Team Lead or specially trained SOS agent**. Do not promise a resolution or refund outcome before the handoff.

**Escalation triggers:**
- Member shares a **severe documented hardship** (illness, death in family, military deployment) and is willing to provide documentation
- Request involves a **discontinued product** (e.g., L2L Alumni Program)
- Member reports a **serious conduct concern** (discrimination, harassment)
- Member demands escalation after a complete, documented save attempt
- Request was previously escalated and denied — route to SOS for Director-level decision

*If unsure whether to escalate:* Post in the SOS channel for coaching first.

**Step 6 — Final processing:**
- If refund is approved (within policy or documented exception): process per the Refund SOP; log all notes in HubSpot
- For Grad Set, plaque, or other graduation items: follow the Enrollment Grad Set SOP
- Update the SOS channel with notes if escalated, including expected follow-up timeframe

**Final checklist before closing:**
- [ ] Save attempt documented in HubSpot ticket
- [ ] FOL requests also logged in MEx FOL Saves Rate Tracker (if applicable)
- [ ] Refund decision aligns with policy or has escalation approval
- [ ] Follow-up sent to member confirming resolution or next steps
- [ ] SOS channel updated if escalated

---

**What qualifies as a save attempt:**
Must include all of the following:
1. **Empathy and Acknowledgment** — let the member know we're sad to hear they're considering canceling
2. **Transparency** — explain you'll check their account and do all you can to support them
3. **Discovery** — ask open-ended questions to understand their reason
4. **Policy Clarity** — clearly explain the 30-day window and what options remain
5. **Value Reinforcement** — share specific benefits (scholarships, leadership tools, discounts, lifetime access)
6. **Issue Resolution** — if the issue can be fixed (tech access, step confusion, missing item), take ownership

Save attempts must be documented in HubSpot (and FOL Saves Tracker when applicable) **before** escalating or processing any refund.

---

**Examples of save language (CARE-based, tailored):**
> *"I'm really glad you reached out — let's figure out what's going on. Can you tell me a little more about what's made this feel like it's not the right fit?"*

> *"I hear you, and I want to make sure we explore everything available before we make any decisions. Based on what you're describing, here's what I think could really help you..."*

> *"Your membership includes lifetime access — even if things are busy right now, it'll be there when you're ready. Let me show you what's available in an online chapter that might fit your schedule better."*

> *"I totally understand — let me walk you through some of the benefits you may not have had a chance to explore yet. A lot of members find the scholarships and career tools are what make it click for them."*

---

**Common questions:**

*Can we issue a refund after 30 days?*
- Only with a documented exception approved by MEX Leadership after a second save attempt by a Team Lead or SOS-trained Specialist. These cases must be rare and involve documented hardship or a discontinued product.

*What qualifies as a documented hardship?*
- Illness, death in the family, military deployment — member must be willing to provide supporting documentation.

*Can we refund the Grad Set or Parent Package?*
- Parent Packages can be refunded at any time. Grad Sets follow the Enrollment Grad Set SOP. Personalized items are non-refundable.

*What if a member's L2L program was discontinued?*
- This qualifies as a discontinued product escalation — route to SOS for Team Lead review.

## Process: ACH Refunds

**When this comes up:** A refund cannot be processed through the original payment method — either because it is no longer available, the member has been with NSLS 120+ days, or an urgent exception was approved.

**Key definitions:**
- **ACH** — Automated Clearing House; electronic bank-to-bank transfer via RAMP (preferred refund method for eligible exceptions)
- **RAMP** — NSLS's financial platform used to send ACH refunds to members

**When to use ACH (vs. original payment method):**
- Original payment method is **no longer available** (expired card, closed PayPal, etc.)
- Refund approved as an exception AND member has been with NSLS **120+ days**
- Urgent refund approved by the MEx Support Manager or Director

**When to use Check (last resort):**
- Member declines ACH and explicitly prefers a paper check
- Any situation where ACH is applicable but member opts out

**Prerequisites:**
- Refund must be approved per the Refund Exception Policy before proceeding
- Refunds within 30 days go back to original payment method unless it's unavailable
- 31–119 days: exception approval required, but can still refund to original method if approved
- 120+ days: must use ACH or check

---

**Step 1 — Confirm ACH eligibility:**
Verify that one of the following applies:
- Original payment method is no longer available
- Refund is an approved exception AND member has been with NSLS 120+ days
- Urgent refund approved by Manager or Director

**Step 2 — Process in Feather and notify Billing:**
1. In Feather's Transactions tab, select **"Check"** as the refund type (ACH is not yet a dropdown option in Feather — "Check" is the placeholder)
2. Inform the member their refund will be **directly deposited into their bank account** via RAMP
3. Email Billing using the **Billing Email Template** in HubSpot — indicate "ACH" in the address section of the template (HubSpot snippet: search "billing" or "RAMP")

**Step 3 — Allow 48 hours for internal processing:**
- RAMP setup and the initial email to the member takes up to **48 hours** after the refund is processed in Feather
- Set expectations with the member: they will receive a RAMP email within 48 hours

**Step 4 — Verify member email address:**
- Before processing, confirm the member's email address in Feather is correct — RAMP sends to the address on file

**Step 5 — Member receives and responds to RAMP email:**
- RAMP emails the member requesting their banking information for direct deposit
- Once the member enters their info, the ACH deposit processes in **3–5 business days**

**Step 6 — Total timeline:**
- Full process (Feather refund → RAMP email → member inputs info → deposit): approximately **7–10 business days**
- Timeline assumes the member responds to RAMP promptly and depends on their bank's processing speed

**Step 7 — If member requests a check instead:**
1. Obtain MEL approval (already required for exception refunds)
2. Deactivate the member in Feather; add a note at the bottom of their Feather profile
3. Email Billing using the **Check Refund email template** (see below for structure):
   - Member name, reason for check refund, who approved it
   - Feather transaction link, HubSpot reference
   - Member's full mailing address
4. Checks are mailed **weekly on Tuesdays**; delivery takes **10–12 business days** via regular mail

---

**Post-procedure:**
- The Director of MEx notifies Team Leads daily about members who received the RAMP email but haven't responded
- Team Leads assign follow-up calls/emails to those members
- Finance closes outstanding ACH requests **30 days** after the RAMP email is sent — if the member hasn't entered banking info by then, a check is mailed to the address on file in Feather

---

**Member communication — key talking points:**

*Confirming ACH method:*
> *"Your refund will be processed via ACH deposit through our trusted partner RAMP. You'll receive an email from them within 48 hours with instructions to enter your banking information. Once entered, expect 3–5 business days for the deposit."*

*Member concerned about RAMP security:*
> *"RAMP is a trusted NSLS partner. They use multi-factor authentication, encryption, tokenization, and SOC 2 compliance. You can review their security at trust.ramp.com. If you'd prefer a check, I can arrange that — though it takes 10–12 business days via regular mail."*

*Member hasn't responded to RAMP email:*
> *"We noticed you haven't entered your banking information yet. Please check for the RAMP email and respond within 5 business days. If we don't hear back, we'll send a paper check to the address we have on file — once mailed, the ACH option is no longer available."*

---

**Common questions:**

*Can ACH be processed immediately?*
- No — allow 48 hours for internal processing before the RAMP email reaches the member, then 3–5 business days after they input banking info. Total: 7–10 business days.

*What if the member's RAMP email goes to spam?*
- Ask them to check their spam folder. If still missing, verify the email address in Feather is correct and confirm with Billing that the RAMP request was submitted.

*What happens if the member never responds to RAMP?*
- Finance closes the ACH request after 30 days and a check is mailed to the address on file.

*Can we process a check refund without going through RAMP first?*
- Only if the member explicitly declines ACH and requests a check. Always offer ACH first.

## Process: A&E Reimbursement — Offer Met

**When this comes up:** A member who joined during the Fall 2024 enrollment test (April 26–October 18, 2024) completed their A&E program within one year and is requesting the reimbursement they were promised at enrollment.

**⚠️ Eligibility is limited:** This offer applies **only** to members who joined between **4/26/2024 and 10/18/2024** during the testing application phase. Not all members who joined during this window received the offer — always verify via the Feather report before proceeding.

**What was offered:** The cost of A&E Certificates and/or A&E/FOL Credits is refunded upon completion within one year of purchase. If a member purchased both, they may receive a reimbursement for one or both, depending on what they completed in time.

**Steps:**

**Step 1 — Verify eligibility in Feather:**
1. Check the member's Feather progress to confirm the certificate portion (at minimum) has been completed
2. Access the **Refundable on Completion report** in Feather
3. Enter the member's email address and set **"Meets Completion Criteria"** to **Yes**
4. If the member appears in the report, proceed to Step 2
5. If the member does **not** appear: verify their progress first — they may not have finished. If progress is complete but they're not on the report, submit a Help Desk tech ticket

**Step 2 — Access transactions and begin refund:**
1. Click **View** on the report to open the member's Feather profile
2. Navigate to the **Transactions** tab
3. When processing the refund, enter **"ENROLLMENT OFFER MET"** as the refund reason

**Step 3 — Process refund based on timing:**
- **Within 120 days** of original payment: refund normally through Feather using the same payment method the member used
- **Outside 120 days** (most common): standard refund is not possible — submit an **ACH/RAMP refund request** to Billing per SOP_MEx ACH Refunds

**Post-procedure:**
- Submit the ACH/RAMP request to Billing on behalf of the member to ensure the refund is fully processed and the loop is closed

**Common questions:**

*Who qualifies for the A&E reimbursement offer?*
- Only members who joined between **April 26, 2024 and October 18, 2024** during the Fall enrollment test. Verify transactions to confirm they received the offer — not everyone in that window did.

*The member is requesting a refund but isn't on the Feather report — what do I do?*
- First verify their Feather progress is actually complete. If it is complete but they're not on the report, submit a Help Desk tech ticket for investigation.

*What is the refund reason to enter in Feather?*
- Always enter **"ENROLLMENT OFFER MET"** — this is required for proper tracking.

*What if it has been more than 120 days?*
- Route through SOP_MEx ACH Refunds for an ACH/RAMP refund via Billing.
