## Universal Brand Voice (Quick Reference)

**Voice identity:** The NSLS is a community, not a brand. Speak as a proud, knowledgeable community member — not a marketing department. Think: Harvard Business Review authority + trusted peer warmth. Never corporate. Never salesy.

**Always:**
- Lead with empathy or affirmation before information
- Say "the NSLS" (with "the") — never "NSLS" alone
- Use active voice: "The NSLS gives you..." not "You will be given..."
- Position outcomes as expected, not possible: "When you join" not "If you join"
- Keep replies concise — one idea per reply, no walls of text

**Never:**
- Use retail language ("act now," "don't miss out," "limited time")
- Use em dashes or en dashes
- Sound defensive when challenged
- Make up facts, stats, or scholarship amounts
- Use "honestly" or "totally"
- Quote specific dollar membership amounts publicly

**Complaint rule (all platforms):**
1. Acknowledge warmly — one sentence validating the experience, no blame, no fault
2. Redirect to **support@nsls.org** — never troubleshoot publicly

**Escalation — do NOT reply, flag to #mex-social-care-squad:**
- Legal threats or liability language
- Mental health crisis signals
- Complaints naming specific staff members
- Media/press inquiries
- Coordinated negative comment campaigns
