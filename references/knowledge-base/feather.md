## Process: Manually Creating a Feather Profile

**When this comes up:** A member cannot self-enroll due to a technical issue or payment problem, and MEX needs to manually create their Feather profile.

**⚠️ Critical rule:** Only use this process when the member is unable to self-enroll. Always confirm the chapter is accepting members before starting.

**Prerequisites:**
- Chapter is active and accepting members
- Invitation/nomination code confirmed
- Payment ready (credit or debit card — no checks, money orders, or paper applications as of August 22, 2025)

**Steps:**

1. **Verify invitation and chapter:**
   - Ask for the invitation/nomination code or look it up in Feather
   - Confirm the chapter is currently accepting members

2. **Create the Feather profile:**
   - Log in to nsls.org → click "Members Area" (top left) → select "Feather"
   - Select "Members" → "Members List" → click the red **"New Member"** button (top right)

3. **Enter member information:**
   - Select the chapter (must be accepting members)
   - Ask if GPA is 3.3 or above — if yes, check the **"Presidential"** box
   - Enter: Salutation, First Name, Last Name
   - **Primary Email** (this becomes their Member ID)
   - **Backup Email** (use same as primary if no second email)
   - Date of Birth in MM/DD/YYYY format
   - Leave Welcome Email checked (auto-selected)

4. **Collect address and contact info:**
   - Street Address, Apartment/Unit (if applicable), City, State, ZIP, Phone Number
   - ⚠️ **Enter only ONE address** (either street OR PO Box — not both). Dual addresses will confuse the system and delay kit delivery.

5. **Enter extra information:**
   - Invitation Code
   - T-shirt Size (select from dropdown)
   - Optional membership add-ons:
     - Core Package: select "LMS FOL $95" or "Lifetime Membership – $95" (unless member qualifies for free membership)
     - Plaque: leave blank if not purchasing
   - **Graduation Set:** Cannot be added in this manual enrollment screen
     - Add the Grad Set cost to the Authorize total before charging
     - After creating the profile, go to the member's Transactions tab and manually add the Graduation Set as a separate transaction

6. **Process payment in Authorize:**
   - Under Payment Type, select "Authorize (manual)"
   - Process payment using SOP: How/When to Charge a Card in Authorize
   - Copy the Transaction ID and paste it into the **Transaction Approval box** in Feather

7. **Ask about opt-outs:**
   - Partner emails
   - SMS/texts
   - Newsletters

8. **Save and confirm access:**
   - Click Save to create the profile
   - Member should receive a Welcome Email within 5 minutes — ask them to check spam/junk if not received
   - You may help them log in by setting up a temporary password

**Post-procedure checklist:**
- [ ] Chapter is accepting members
- [ ] Invitation code verified
- [ ] Payment processed through Authorize
- [ ] Transaction added in Feather (including IKF if required and any add-ons)
- [ ] Correct Core Package selected (FOL or LMS FOL)
- [ ] Email the member a copy of the transaction receipt

**Common questions:**

*When should MEX manually create a profile?*
- Only if the member is unable to self-enroll due to technical issues or payment problems.

*Can we add a Graduation Set during manual enrollment?*
- Not directly. Add the Grad Set cost to the Authorize total, then once the profile is created, add the Graduation Set as a separate transaction in Feather's Transactions tab.

*Can we accept checks or money orders?*
- No. As of August 22, 2025, all payments must be made by debit/credit card, PayPal, Google Pay, or manually via Authorize. No checks, money orders, or paper applications.

## Process: Chapter Transfer

**When this comes up:** A member needs or requests a transfer to a different chapter — due to a school change, inactive chapter, scheduling needs, A&E certification access, or wanting their new school's name on their certificate.

**Key rule:** Only transfer to a chapter that is **active AND accepting members**. Never transfer into an inactive or closed chapter.

**When transfers apply:**
- Member changed schools and wants to continue their NSLS journey at their new institution
- Original chapter is inactive — no Chapter Leaders to review steps, blocking progress, induction kits, or certificates
- Member needs to complete A&E certifications (inactive chapter may prevent this)
- Member wants their new school's name reflected on certificates
- Member needs more scheduling flexibility → Online Chapter transfers allow fully self-paced completion

**When transfers do NOT apply:**
- Member is already fully inducted and not pursuing additional certifications — no transfer needed
- If flexibility is the concern, encourage member to contact their Chapter Leaders about online accommodations before transferring

**Note on fees:**
- If the original chapter covered the Induction Kit Fee (IKF) but the new chapter does not (e.g., SNHU or the National Online Chapter), the member must pay the **$5.99 IKF**
- Transferring to SNHU before induction: member can choose between a t-shirt or cords. If they bought a standard Graduation Set at enrollment, they'll receive the standard set (cords), not the SNHU enrollment version (tassel charm)

**Steps:**

**Step 1 — Confirm chapter is active and accepting members:**
1. Search for the chapter in Feather → check **Status** (Active/Inactive)
2. Click on the school → view **Enrollment Setting** section → check **Accepting Members**:
   - Green "Yes" = accepting members
   - Red "No" = not accepting members
3. **Do not transfer into a chapter that is not accepting members**

**Step 2 — Transfer the member in Feather:**
1. Open the member's Feather profile → select the **Edit** tab
2. Find **School Information** → click **"Transfer to other Chapter"**
3. A "Transfer Chapter" pop-up will appear — type the chapter name and select from the dropdown
4. Click the red **"Transfer Member"** button
5. Member should see the change in their Dashboard immediately

⚠️ **Under no circumstances should you select "National Office"** — that chapter is for employee use only.

**Common questions:**

*What if the new chapter is not accepting members?*
- Let the member know the chapter is not currently accepting. They can stay in their current chapter or wait until the new one reopens.

*What if the chapter is inactive?*
- If the chapter is inactive but the member is enrolled at the school, they have the option to start a new chapter through the NSLS Internship Program. Share the "Start a Chapter" link with them — a National Office Representative will follow up.

*Can a member transfer after being fully inducted?*
- Generally no transfer is needed if fully inducted and not pursuing additional certifications. If they still want to transfer, check if the new chapter is accepting members and proceed with Step 2.

## Process: Credit for a Step

**When this comes up:** A member completed a Speaker Broadcast step but it is not reflected in their progress — typically because they attended via an online chapter or viewed an archived broadcast.

**Key rule:** Credit can only be given for **3 different** Speaker Broadcasts, each at least **30 minutes** long. Archived broadcasts do **not** count toward step credit.

**Steps:**

1. **Confirm chapter type** — verify whether the member is in an Online or Standard chapter

2. **Acknowledge and confirm which broadcast they attended:**
   - Ask the member which specific broadcast they are requesting credit for (date, title, or speaker if known)

3. **Ask a friendly question about the content:**
   - Ask a brief, genuine question about something from the broadcast to confirm the member actually attended and engaged (e.g., "What was one thing you took away from the session?")

4. **Evaluate the response:**
   - Ensure the member gives a genuine, content-relevant response
   - Archived broadcasts do **not** count — if the member watched an archived recording rather than attending live, credit cannot be applied

5. **Assign credit in Feather:**
   - Navigate to the member's Feather profile → **Progress** tab
   - Use the **"Assign Progress Completion"** option to apply credit for the applicable broadcast step

6. **Inform the member:**
   - Let them know the credit has been applied and they can view the update in their member dashboard

**Common questions:**

*How many Speaker Broadcasts does a member need?*
- Three different Speaker Broadcasts, each at least 30 minutes long.

*Can credit be given for an archived broadcast?*
- No. Only live broadcasts count toward the Speaker Broadcast induction step.

*What if the member can't name the broadcast content?*
- Do not apply credit if the member cannot demonstrate they attended. Escalate to MEX reviewer.

**Escalation:** Route to MEX reviewer for unusual credit requests or if the member disputes denial.

## Process: Tech Issues with Videos in Members Area

**When this comes up:** A member reports a video isn't playing as part of the Speaker Broadcast step in the Foundations of Leadership (FOL) program. This can block their progress — respond promptly and with empathy.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify chapter type** — verify whether the member is in an Online or Standard chapter (note: some Standard chapters conduct meetings virtually)

2. **Confirm browser** — member must be using **Google Chrome** or **Mozilla Firefox** only

3. **Check browser version** — ask the member to verify they have the most recent version of Chrome or Firefox; updating can resolve some tech issues

4. **Confirm system updates** — ask if all system updates are current on the device they're using; if not, they need to update first

5. **Masquerade to test** — if the issue persists after steps 1–4:
   - Masquerade as the member in Feather
   - Attempt to play the specific video they're having trouble with
   - If you can replicate the issue, it may be a widespread error

6. **Device troubleshooting** — if the video plays fine when you masquerade:
   - Instruct the member to try a different device using Chrome or Firefox
   - PCs work best, but tablets, phones, and other devices with compatible browsers can also work

7. **Escalate to tech** — if the error persists after all of the above:
   - Fill out the **MEx Tech Issue Tab** to create a tech ticket for further investigation
   - Follow: SOP-MEx Tech Issue Submission Process 2025

**Common questions:**

*Video works when I masquerade but not for the member — what do I do?*
- Have the member try a different browser or device (Chrome or Firefox, latest version). Ask them to take screenshots and share them in the MEx Tech Issue Tab.

*When should I escalate in #sos-escalation vs the Tech Issue Track / Help Desk?*
- Use **`#sos-escalation`** if you're unsure about how to proceed or want advice from a peer or Team Lead
- Use **`#help-desk`** / **Tech Issue spreadsheet** only when you need the **Tech Team** to create or review a ticket

**Resources:**
- [SOP-MEx Tech Issue Submission Process 2025](https://drive.google.com/open?id=19NgEWXFvb4f873SqBrNXSnd1RAfXy19iDi69yEZOVJQ&usp=drive_copy)

## Process: Disabled Member Wants to Rejoin

**When this comes up:** A member whose profile was disabled (due to a refund, cancellation, or other reason) wants to reactivate or rejoin NSLS.

**Context:** When a profile is disabled, the member can no longer log in with their original email. Reactivation options depend on timing. The original email address cannot be reused as a Member ID — but it can be added as a backup email to a new account.

---

**Step 1 — Same-day refund and member changed their mind:**
1. Notify your manager or senior MEX specialist via DM to request a void
2. Once approved, void the refund and reactivate the account in Feather
3. Confirm with the member that their original email can now be used to log in

> *Suggested language:* "I'm happy to hear you want to reactivate your membership! Since your refund was requested today, I'll reach out to a manager to cancel it and get your account reactivated."

---

**Step 2 — Refund or deactivation was NOT same-day (more than 1 day ago):**
1. Go to the member's school chapter in Feather — confirm the chapter is **Active and Accepting Members**
2. If accepting: share the chapter's general nomination code
3. Have the member register again at nsls.org using a **new email address**
   - ⚠️ The original email cannot be reused as a Member ID. It can be added as a backup email on the new account.

> *Suggested language:* "Since your account was closed more than a day ago, you'll need to re-enroll using a new email. Here's your chapter's code: [Insert Code]. The email from your previous account can't be reused, but you're welcome to add it as a backup email."

---

**Step 3 — Over one week since deactivation:**
- Treat as a new enrollment
- Use the school's general nomination code (found in Feather → Chapter View)
- Set up a new membership from scratch

---

**Post-procedure (HubSpot):**
1. Log the interaction — Title: `Returning Member Rejoin Request` | Category: Member Benefits → Membership Reactivation
2. Note if a void was submitted and approved by a senior or manager
3. Note which chapter code was shared and whether the new account was created
4. Set a task reminder if follow-up is needed (e.g., waiting for manager void confirmation)

**Common questions:**

*Can the member reuse their original email?*
- No — once a membership is disabled, the original email cannot be reused for enrollment. A new email is required. The original can be set as a backup email.

*What if they try to enroll with the same email?*
- They'll get an error. Advise them to use a new email and add the old one as a backup.

*Can we restore an old account after several days?*
- Only if the refund is from the same day and approved for voiding. Otherwise, they must rejoin as a new member with a new email.

**Escalation:** Void requests require manager or senior MEX specialist approval — always notify via DM before proceeding.

## Process: Invitations Report in Feather

**When this comes up:** A member asks about their chapter's nomination deadline, or MEX needs to look up an invitation deadline to answer a nominee inquiry.

**Key definition — Nomination Deadline:** The date by which eligible students must be enrolled to join a specific chapter. May be extended in some cases (except SNHU — see below).

**Before pulling the report:** Check whether the member reaching out already has an NSLS membership. Members who are already registered do not need to register again or pay additional dues, regardless of any invitation or deadline emails they receive.

**Steps:**

1. **Navigate to Feather CRM** → select **Chapters** from the left-hand navigation menu → under **Logistics**, select **Invitations**

2. **Set the date range** on the Reports - Invitations page:
   - Enter a **Start Date** and **End Date** to limit the search to the current invitation cycle (not past cycles)
   - End date should be **at least 2 months** from the date the report is being pulled

3. **Select the chapter** from the Chapter drop-down menu → click the red **"Run Report"** button
   - All other fields are optional

4. **Locate the invitation deadline** — scroll to the bottom of the results page → select the chapter type (**Standard** or **Online**) → Feather will display the invitation deadline

**Post-procedure — if the member needs a deadline extension:**
- Direct the member to contact their **Chapter Advisor** to request an extension
- If the member doesn't know their advisor: search the chapter in Feather → open the chapter page → scroll down to the **"Invitation Contact"** box on the left
- The correct contact for deadline extensions and fee waivers is the advisor with the **non-@nsls.org email address** (e.g., `acadhonorsociety@snhu.edu`, not `snhu@nsls.org`)

⚠️ **SNHU exception:** Deadline extensions will **not** be granted for SNHU once the deadline has passed. SNHU has strict deadlines — no exceptions.
