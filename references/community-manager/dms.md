# DMs Voice Guide (Instagram, Facebook, X)

## Platform Role

DMs are the modern-day help desk. They're private conversations between the NSLS and individual users (usually members or prospective members). Because they're private, the tone can be warmer and more conversational than public comments.

## What to Expect in DMs

**Instagram and Facebook DMs predominantly include:**
- Story tags (induction kits especially)
- Story reactions
- Questions from members about specific programming or induction kit updates
- Questions from prospective members about joining the NSLS (especially during peak invitation season)
- Responses to Orientation, LTD, and Speaker Broadcast social media engagement questions (NOTE: Seeing "#theNSLS" is a clue that it's related to event engagement)

**X DMs are lower volume and predominantly include:**
- FOL (Foundations of Leadership) reflections
- Personal achievements or stories
- Questions or issues
- Solicitors (ignore these)

## When to Respond

**Generally always.** Exceptions:
- Spam and scams (e.g., "Your account will be shut down in 30 days if you don't...")
- Solicitors (e.g., "Hire me/subscribe to my service so we can help you grow your socials")
- Story reactions (not necessary to respond)
- Messages that are irrelevant and don't make sense

## How to Respond

DMs are conversational and casual. Imagine you're texting a new professional work friend you really want to impress.

**Core principles:**
- **Be supportive and helpful.** Remind members of NSLS resources, events, and benefits whenever relevant. Share links when you can.
- **Be curious.** Ask follow-up questions to keep the conversation going.
- **Keep it brief and straightforward.** No walls of text.
- **Be authentic and empathetic.** Communicate like you're talking to a friend.
- **Casual and fun is encouraged,** but balance with professionalism.

## Tone

The most casual of all channels. Conversational, warm, personal. Still professional (proper spelling, grammar), but the energy is friendly and approachable. Emojis are welcome and encouraged where they fit naturally.

## Scenario Guidelines

### Story Tags (Induction kits, events, milestones)
- Reshare the story to the NSLS account
- Send a warm, brief message acknowledging what they shared
- If it's a particularly compelling story, flag it to #mex-social-care-squad for potential marketing use

### Questions About Programming or Membership
- Answer warmly and directly
- Share relevant links (NSLS.org, specific program pages)
- If you're unsure of the answer, let them know you'll find out and get back to them (then actually follow up)
- For complex issues, redirect to support@nsls.org

### Prospective Member Questions
- Be encouraging but not pushy
- Answer their specific question
- Frame the NSLS as a community and opportunity, not a product
- Point them to NSLS.org for enrollment details

### Personal Achievements or Stories (especially on X)
- Congratulate them warmly
- Share with Michael Adamian or Gabriela so the team can explore featuring them
- Keep it personal and genuine

### Issues or Complaints in DMs
- Take it seriously, be empathetic
- Work with them to understand the issue
- For anything that needs formal resolution, redirect to support@nsls.org
- Never dismiss or minimize their concern

### Solicitors and Spam
- Ignore completely. Do not respond.

## Hard Rules for DMs

- Never share internal information or confidential data
- Never make promises the NSLS can't keep (e.g., guaranteed outcomes, specific scholarship amounts)
- Never quote specific membership fees
- If something feels like a brand risk or escalation situation, flag to #mex-social-care-squad before responding
- Never ignore a legitimate question or concern, even if it's awkward
- Keep the universal brand rules in mind, even in casual DMs (active voice, no retail language, etc.)
