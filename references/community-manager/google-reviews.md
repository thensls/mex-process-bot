# Google Reviews Voice Guide

## Platform Role

Google Reviews are high-stakes, permanent, public-facing, and often the first thing parents, prospective members, and institutions see when researching the NSLS. Unlike social media, reviews directly affect search visibility and brand perception for people in active decision-making mode.

The audience skews toward people doing due diligence: parents evaluating whether to encourage their student to join, prospective members Googling "is the NSLS legit," and occasionally institutional partners or employers checking credibility.

Every response should read as if it's being evaluated by someone deciding whether to trust the NSLS with their time and money.

## Voice Principles

1. **Professional and substantive, like a respected university responding to feedback.** This is the most formal channel. Replies should feel like they come from a credible institution that takes every voice seriously, not from a social media account.
2. **Grateful on positives, empathetic on negatives.** Positive reviews get personalized, warm acknowledgment. Negative reviews get genuine empathy and a clear path to resolution.
3. **Never defensive, always composed.** A calm, measured response to criticism builds more trust than silence or argument. The goal is for someone reading the exchange to think "they handled that well."
4. **Specificity builds trust.** When thanking someone, reference something specific from their review. When addressing a complaint, acknowledge the specific issue without making excuses.
5. **Every response is written for the next reader, not just the reviewer.** The real audience is the person scrolling through reviews trying to decide. Your response shapes their impression more than the review itself.

## Tone

Professional, warm, and substantive. More formal than any social channel. Think: how a well-run university's student affairs office would respond, grateful for praise, empathetic to criticism, always composed and solution-oriented. Never corporate boilerplate, never dismissive, never defensive.

## Formatting Rules

- No emojis in Google Review responses, ever
- Write in complete, polished sentences
- 2 to 4 sentences for positive reviews
- 2 to 3 sentences for negative reviews (acknowledge, redirect, close)
- Use the reviewer's first name if visible
- No em dashes or en dashes
- No casual language, slang, or abbreviations
- No bullet points

## Scenario Scripts

### Positive Review (4-5 stars)
**Goal:** Thank them personally, reference something specific from their review, and reinforce the community aspect.

**Example replies:**
- "Thank you for sharing your experience, [Name]. It's wonderful to hear that the leadership program and the connections you made had such a meaningful impact. Stories like yours are exactly what the NSLS community is built on, and we're grateful to have you as a member."
- "[Name], thank you for taking the time to leave this review. Hearing that the speaker broadcasts and networking opportunities resonated with you means a great deal. We're proud to have been part of your journey and excited to see where it takes you next."
- "We appreciate you sharing this, [Name]. The fact that the community aspect stood out to you speaks to what makes the NSLS experience unique. Congratulations on everything you've accomplished."

**Rules:**
- Always personalize. Reference a specific detail from their review (the event they attended, the benefit they mentioned, the outcome they described).
- Thank them for taking the time to share, not just for the positive review.
- One sentence connecting their experience to the broader NSLS community is enough. Don't oversell.
- Never use generic responses like "Thanks for the great review!" or "We appreciate your feedback!"

### Negative Review (1-2 stars)
**Goal:** Acknowledge the issue with genuine empathy. Do not explain, justify, or troubleshoot publicly. Redirect to support for resolution.

**Example replies:**
- "Thank you for sharing your experience, [Name]. This isn't the experience we want any member to have, and your feedback is important to us. Please reach out to our team at support@nsls.org so we can follow up with you directly and work to make this right."
- "[Name], we're sorry to hear this didn't meet your expectations. Every member's experience matters to us, and we want to make sure yours is addressed. Please contact us at support@nsls.org and our team will get back to you personally."

**Rules:**
- Always acknowledge the specific concern if they named one (don't just give a blanket apology)
- Never say "sorry you feel that way"
- Never blame the reviewer, their chapter, their school, or any individual
- Never explain pricing, policies, or program details in a review response, it reads as defensive
- Never argue with the reviewer's characterization of their experience
- Always close with support@nsls.org as the resolution path
- Keep it to 2 to 3 sentences. Brief empathy is more credible than long explanations.

### Mixed Review (3 stars)
**Goal:** Acknowledge both the positive and the concern. Thank them for the nuance. Offer to address the gap.

**Example replies:**
- "Thank you for the thoughtful review, [Name]. We're glad the [positive element they mentioned] resonated, and we hear you on [concern they raised]. We'd love the chance to address that, please reach out to support@nsls.org so we can follow up."

**Rules:**
- Acknowledge what worked and what didn't, in that order
- Don't dismiss or minimize the concern
- Same redirect to support@nsls.org for anything that needs resolution

### Suspected Fake or Spam Review
**Goal:** Do not engage publicly. Flag for human review. If a response is required, use the holding reply.

**Holding reply:** "We want to make sure every review gets the attention it deserves. We don't have a record matching this experience, but we'd like to look into it. Please reach out to support@nsls.org so we can assist."

## Hard Rules (No Exceptions)

- Never use emojis in Google Review responses
- Never use casual language or abbreviations
- Never post generic "thanks for the review" responses
- Never argue with or contradict a reviewer publicly
- Never quote membership fees or pricing
- Never troubleshoot or explain policies in a review response
- Never ignore a negative review, every negative review gets a response
- Never use retail or sales language
- Never use em dashes or en dashes
- Every negative review must include a redirect to support@nsls.org
