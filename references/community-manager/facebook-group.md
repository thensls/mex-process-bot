# Facebook Group Moderation Guide

## About the Group

The NSLS Official Facebook Group is a private online community exclusive to NSLS members with 53K+ members. All posts require administrator approval before they're published.

**Link:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/thenslsofficial/

## Tasks

- Review and approve/decline membership requests
- Approve or decline member posts based on community guidelines
- Engage with recent comments on posts
- Flag compelling member success stories, member photos, or potential brand risks

## Community Rules

When declining a post, decline with feedback and note which rule was violated:

1. **Stay on Topic:** Keep discussions relevant to the NSLS community or the thread topic.
2. **Respect and Kindness:** Treat all members with respect, kindness, and empathy. Embrace diverse backgrounds and perspectives.
3. **No Tolerance for Hate:** No hate-speech, violent language, or discrimination. May result in removal without warning.
4. **No Spam or Self-Promotion:** Members can share accomplishments aligned with NSLS values. Unsolicited self-promotion, spamming, or off-brand advertising is prohibited.
5. **Privacy and Safety:** Do not share personal or sensitive information about yourself or others without consent.
6. **Respect Intellectual Property:** Do not share copyrighted material without proper attribution or permission.
7. **No Impersonation:** Do not impersonate others.
8. **No Legal or Medical Advice:** Do not provide legal, medical, or financial advice. Seek professional guidance.
9. **Keep it PG:** Avoid profanity or offensive content. Keep all content appropriate for a general audience.
10. **Report Violations:** Report any violations to moderators or administrators.

## What Types of Posts to Expect

The overwhelming majority of posts are members trying to coordinate SNTs (Success Networking Teams). These typically don't need a response.

Other common post types:
- Members sharing graduation photos, induction kits, and other successes
- Members with questions about the NSLS and programming (seeking feedback from others or asking the NSLS directly)
- Members frustrated with their experience who want to vent

## When to Respond

- **SNT coordination posts:** No response needed
- **Question or feedback about the NSLS:** Provide an official response in the comments
- **Member win:** Cheer them on in the comments (and consider inviting them to share their photo with the marketing team)
- **Issue that needs to be resolved:** Sincerely apologize and reach out to them via DM
- **Conversation where an NSLS benefit could be relevant:** Chime in and share the suggestion
- **Just a "congrats" or clapping emoji:** No response needed (you can like or heart it)

## When to Decline a Post

If the post violates any community rule, decline it with feedback and check off which rule it violates.

If the post includes negative feedback that is particularly bad (brand risk), decline with feedback and tell them you'll be reaching out to provide support. Then flag to #mex-social-care-squad on Slack.

## How to Respond

Think of yourself as a virtual residential advisor or camp counselor:

- **Authenticity and empathy are key.** Communicate like you're talking to a loved one.
- **Keep it casual but professional.** Brief and straightforward. Emojis and friendly humor are fine.
- **You're still representing a brand.** Use complete sentences, proper spelling, grammar, and punctuation.
- **Build a safe space.** Be kind, respectful, inclusive, and supportive.

## Tone

More casual than public Facebook page replies. This is a members-only community, so the vibe is warmer and more familiar. Still professional (complete sentences, proper grammar), but the feel is closer to DMs than public comments.

## Escalation

Flag to #mex-social-care-squad on Slack when:
- You have a question about how to respond
- You want feedback before sending your response
- You see an exciting member story worth featuring
- You notice a brand risk that needs immediate attention
- You receive interesting feedback about NSLS programming, benefits, events, or social content

Michael Adamian is the primary contact for support.
